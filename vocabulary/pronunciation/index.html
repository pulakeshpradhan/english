    English Pronunciation

    Pronunciation is often the biggest barrier to fluency. Even if your grammar is perfect, incorrect pronunciation can make you hard to understand.

    Common Pronunciation Challenges

    1. Th Sound: The 'th' sound can be voiced (as in this) or unvoiced (as in think).
    2. R vs. L: Particularly difficult for speakers of Asian languages.
    3. V vs. W: The 'v' sound requires top teeth specifically on the bottom lip.
    4. Silent Letters: English has many silent letters (e.g., knight, psychology, doubt).

    Tips for Better Pronunciation

    • Shadowing: Listen to a native speaker and repeat exactly what they say.
    • Record Yourself: Compare your recording to the original.
    • Focus on Stress: English is a stress-timed language. Word stress changes the meaning.
