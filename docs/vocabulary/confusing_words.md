# Confusing Words

Keep these pairs straight!

## Common Confusions

### 1. Its vs. It's

**Its**: Possessive form (The cat licked its paw).
**It's**: Contraction of "it is" (It's raining).

### 2. Affect vs. Effect

**Affect**: Usually a verb (The weather affected my mood).
**Effect**: Usually a noun (The medication had a side effect).

### 3. Then vs. Than

**Then**: Time or sequence (First we ate, then we slept).
**Than**: Comparison (She is taller than him).

### 4. Lose vs. Loose

**Lose**: To fail to keep (Don't lose your keys).
**Loose**: Not tight (My belt is loose).

### 5. Complimentary vs. Complementary

**Complimentary**: Free or praising.
**Complementary**: Completing or enhancing (Wine is complementary to cheese).
